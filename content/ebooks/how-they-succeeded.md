In *How They Succeeded*, Orison Swett Marden — the founder of SUCCESS Magazine — presents **firsthand interviews and stories** of some of the most successful Americans of the late 19th century. From inventors to business leaders, each chapter reveals the habits, mindsets, and principles that drove extraordinary achievement.

Rather than offering abstract theory, Marden lets the achievers speak for themselves, creating a practical collection of real-world wisdom about what it actually takes to succeed.

## What You'll Learn

- Real strategies used by self-made millionaires and industry leaders
- Common habits and mindsets shared by successful people
- How persistence and character outweigh talent and luck
- Practical advice on building a career from nothing
- The role of education, hard work, and opportunity in success

## Why This Book Matters

Orison Swett Marden is considered the **father of the modern success movement**. His work directly influenced Napoleon Hill, Dale Carnegie, and virtually every motivational author who followed. *How They Succeeded* is particularly valuable because it captures the wisdom of real achievers in their own words — making it a precursor to modern interview-based business books like *Tools of Titans*.
