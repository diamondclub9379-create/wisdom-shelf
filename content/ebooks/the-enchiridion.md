*The Enchiridion* (meaning "handbook" in Greek) is a **practical pocket guide to Stoic philosophy**, distilled from the teachings of Epictetus — a former slave who became one of the greatest philosophers of the Roman Empire. At just 53 short chapters, it is the most concise and actionable introduction to Stoicism ever compiled.

The book's opening line sets the tone: "Some things are within our power, while others are not." From this simple distinction, Epictetus builds an entire framework for emotional resilience and inner freedom.

## What You'll Learn

- The fundamental Stoic distinction: what you can and cannot control
- How to free yourself from anger, anxiety, and frustration
- Why external events don't upset you — your judgments about them do
- Practical techniques for maintaining composure in any situation
- How to live with integrity regardless of circumstances

## Why This Book Matters

If Marcus Aurelius's *Meditations* is the Stoic journal, *The Enchiridion* is the **Stoic field manual**. It's shorter, more direct, and purely practical. Modern Stoicism advocates like Ryan Holiday and Tim Ferriss frequently recommend it as the best starting point for Stoic philosophy. Navy SEAL James Stockdale credited it with helping him survive seven years as a prisoner of war in Vietnam.
