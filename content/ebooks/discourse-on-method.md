René Descartes' *Discourse on the Method* is the book that **launched modern Western philosophy**. Published in 1637, it introduces Descartes' revolutionary approach to knowledge: doubt everything until you find something that cannot be doubted. This process led him to his famous conclusion — *"Cogito, ergo sum"* (I think, therefore I am).

But this book is more than just that famous phrase. It is a practical guide to **thinking clearly**, avoiding error, and using reason to discover truth — skills as valuable today as they were four centuries ago.

## What You'll Learn

- Descartes' four rules for clear, logical thinking
- How to question assumptions systematically
- The relationship between doubt and certainty
- How rational thinking applies to science and everyday life
- Why "I think, therefore I am" is the foundation of modern philosophy

## Why This Book Matters

*Discourse on the Method* is considered the **starting point of modern philosophy** and a key text in the development of the scientific method. Descartes' emphasis on systematic doubt and rational inquiry shaped the Enlightenment and continues to influence how we think about knowledge, science, and truth. Short, readable, and profoundly influential — it changed the course of Western civilization.
