Adam Smith's *The Wealth of Nations*, published in 1776, is the book that **invented modern economics**. In it, Smith explains how free markets work, why nations prosper or fail, and how the self-interest of individuals can benefit society as a whole — his famous concept of the "invisible hand."

This monumental work covers everything from the division of labor and the nature of money to international trade and the role of government in the economy.

## What You'll Learn

- How the division of labor creates wealth and productivity
- Why free markets are more efficient than controlled economies
- The role of self-interest and competition in driving prosperity
- How money, prices, and wages actually work
- The proper role of government in economic life
- Why some nations are rich and others are poor

## Why This Book Matters

*The Wealth of Nations* is one of the most important books ever written. It directly influenced the founding of the United States, shaped the development of capitalism, and remains the starting point for understanding how economies function. Every economics student studies it; every business leader benefits from understanding its core ideas. Reading Smith gives you the foundation for understanding every economic debate that has followed.
