Wallace D. Wattles wrote *The Science of Getting Rich* in 1910, presenting wealth creation not as a matter of luck or talent, but as an **exact science** that anyone can follow. This short, no-nonsense book lays out a systematic approach to thinking and acting in a way that produces financial abundance.

Wattles argues that getting rich is not about competition or taking from others — it is about creation. By thinking in a "Certain Way" and taking efficient action, anyone can attract wealth regardless of their starting circumstances.

## What You'll Learn

- The fundamental principle behind all wealth creation
- How to think in the "Certain Way" that attracts riches
- Why gratitude is essential to financial success
- How to act with purpose and efficiency
- The difference between competitive and creative thinking

## Why This Book Matters

*The Science of Getting Rich* directly inspired Rhonda Byrne's *The Secret* and remains one of the foundational texts of the New Thought movement. Its principles align closely with modern concepts in positive psychology and goal-setting theory. Despite being over a century old, its advice on focused intention and purposeful action feels remarkably current.

A must-read for anyone interested in the intersection of mindset, wealth, and personal power.
