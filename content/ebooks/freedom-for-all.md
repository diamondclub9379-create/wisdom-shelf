*Freedom for All* is Neville Goddard's declaration that **true freedom is not political or economic — it is psychological**. Published in 1942 during World War II, this book argues that the only prison is the one you build in your own mind, and the only liberation comes from changing your inner assumptions about yourself and the world.

Goddard uses the biblical story of Exodus as a metaphor for personal liberation — Egypt represents your current limiting state, and the Promised Land represents the life you can create through conscious imagination.

## What You'll Learn

- Why true freedom begins in consciousness, not circumstances
- How the story of Exodus is a map for personal transformation
- The technique of "living in the end" — assuming your desire is already fulfilled
- How to break free from limiting beliefs and self-imposed bondage
- Why imagination is the ultimate tool of liberation

## Why This Book Matters

Written during one of humanity's darkest periods, *Freedom for All* carries a timeless message: no external force can imprison a mind that knows how to direct its own imagination. This book is particularly relevant for anyone who feels stuck or trapped by their circumstances and is ready to discover that the key to freedom has been within them all along.
