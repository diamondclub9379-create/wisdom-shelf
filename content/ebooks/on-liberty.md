John Stuart Mill's *On Liberty* is the **most powerful defense of individual freedom** ever written. Published in 1859, it argues that society and government should never restrict a person's liberty unless their actions cause direct harm to others — the famous "harm principle."

Mill makes the case for free speech, freedom of thought, and individual autonomy with a clarity and force that remains unmatched. His arguments are as relevant to today's debates about censorship and individual rights as they were in Victorian England.

## What You'll Learn

- The harm principle: the only legitimate reason to limit someone's freedom
- Why free speech is essential — even for opinions we find wrong or offensive
- How the "tyranny of the majority" can be as dangerous as government oppression
- Why individuality and nonconformity are vital to human progress
- The proper limits of society's authority over the individual

## Why This Book Matters

*On Liberty* is **required reading in political science, law, and philosophy programs worldwide**. Its ideas directly shaped modern concepts of civil liberties, free speech, and human rights. Whether you're interested in politics, law, ethics, or simply understanding why freedom matters, Mill's arguments provide the essential foundation.
