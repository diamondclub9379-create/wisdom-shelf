*The Power of Concentration* is a systematic training manual for developing **mental focus and willpower**. Written in 1918 by Theron Q. Dumont (a pen name of William Walker Atkinson), this book provides 20 practical lessons designed to strengthen your ability to concentrate on any task.

In an era of constant distraction, the ability to focus deeply has become one of the most valuable skills a person can develop. This century-old book addresses that exact challenge with exercises that remain remarkably effective today.

## What You'll Learn

- 20 progressive exercises to build concentration power
- How to eliminate mental distractions and wandering thoughts
- Techniques for developing an iron will
- How concentration affects memory, creativity, and decision-making
- The connection between focused thought and achievement

## Why This Book Matters

Long before Cal Newport wrote *Deep Work* or apps like Forest promised to cure digital distraction, Dumont laid out a complete system for training the mind to focus. The exercises in this book are practical, progressive, and surprisingly modern in their approach. If you struggle with focus or procrastination, this is one of the most useful books you'll ever read — and it's free.
