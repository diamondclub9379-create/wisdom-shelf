Neville Goddard had a gift for distilling profound truths into unforgettable sentences. His quotes continue to inspire millions of people decades after his death. Here are 20 of his most powerful quotes, with context and explanation for each.

## On Imagination

### 1. "Imagination is the beginning of creation."
*From [Awakened Imagination](/ebooks/awakened-imagination/)*

Everything that exists was first imagined. The chair you're sitting in, the building around you, the device you're reading this on — all began as images in someone's mind. Neville's point: this process isn't limited to inventors. *You* create your personal reality the same way.

### 2. "Assume the feeling of the wish fulfilled."
*From [Feeling is the Secret](/ebooks/feeling-is-the-secret/)*

This is Neville's most famous instruction. Not "think about" your wish. Not "hope for" your wish. *Assume the feeling* of already having it. The feeling is the secret ingredient that transforms a thought into a creative force.

### 3. "An assumption, though false, if persisted in, will harden into fact."
*From [The Power of Awareness](/ebooks/the-power-of-awareness/)*

This is the Law of Assumption in one sentence. It doesn't matter if your assumption seems false right now. If you sustain it long enough, reality must conform. This works for both positive and negative assumptions — which is why guarding your inner state matters so much.

### 4. "Change your conception of yourself and you will automatically change the world in which you live."
*From [The Power of Awareness](/ebooks/the-power-of-awareness/)*

You don't need to change your circumstances directly. Change who you believe yourself to be, and the circumstances will rearrange to match. This is why affirmations about *being* ("I am successful") are more powerful than affirmations about *having* ("I have success").

### 5. "Man's chief delusion is his conviction that there are causes other than his own state of consciousness."
*From [The Power of Awareness](/ebooks/the-power-of-awareness/)*

Most people blame external causes for their problems: the economy, their parents, their boss, bad luck. Neville says all of these are effects, not causes. The only cause is your state of consciousness.

## On Consciousness and Reality

### 6. "Consciousness is the only reality."
*From [At Your Command](/ebooks/at-your-command/)*

Neville's most fundamental statement. The physical world is not the primary reality — consciousness is. Matter is a manifestation of consciousness, not the other way around.

### 7. "You are already that which you want to be, and your refusal to believe this is the only reason you do not see it."
*From [At Your Command](/ebooks/at-your-command/)*

Your desired state already exists as a possibility within consciousness. You don't need to *create* it — you need to *accept* it. The gap between where you are and where you want to be is not a gap of action but a gap of belief.

### 8. "Do not try to change people; they are only messengers telling you who you are."
*From his lectures*

Other people reflect your assumptions back to you. If people treat you poorly, examine what you're assuming about yourself. Change the assumption, and their behavior will change — because they were never acting independently of your consciousness.

### 9. "Nothing comes from without. All things come from within."
*From [Your Faith is Your Fortune](/ebooks/your-faith-is-your-fortune/)*

Every event in your life originated in your own consciousness. Good fortune didn't "happen to" you — it came *from* you. So did your challenges. This is both humbling and empowering.

### 10. "The world is a mirror of your assumptions."
*From [Freedom for All](/ebooks/freedom-for-all/)*

Look at your life honestly. What you see around you is a perfect reflection of what you have assumed to be true. If you don't like what you see, don't blame the mirror — change the face (your inner state).

## On Technique and Practice

### 11. "Feeling is the secret."
*From [Feeling is the Secret](/ebooks/feeling-is-the-secret/)*

The title of his most concise book and his most essential teaching. Not visualization alone. Not affirmation alone. The *feeling* — the emotional, sensory experience of your desire fulfilled — is what impresses the subconscious and creates reality.

### 12. "Sleep conceals the creative act while the objective world reveals it."
*From [Feeling is the Secret](/ebooks/feeling-is-the-secret/)*

What you feel as you fall asleep is handed directly to your subconscious. The subconscious then works through the night to manifest it. This is why Neville's primary technique involves falling asleep in the wish fulfilled.

### 13. "Think FROM the end, not OF the end."
*From his lectures*

Thinking *of* your desire keeps it at a distance — it's something you want but don't have. Thinking *from* the end means placing yourself mentally *in* the fulfilled state and looking back. It's the difference between looking at a mountain and standing on its peak.

### 14. "To be desired state, you must create an experience of that state."
*From [Prayer: The Art of Believing](/ebooks/prayer-the-art-of-believing/)*

You cannot manifest what you cannot *experience* internally. If you can't feel wealthy in your imagination, you can't become wealthy in reality. The inner experience must come first.

### 15. "Disregard appearances, conditions, in fact all evidence of your senses that deny the fulfillment of your desire."
*From [The Power of Awareness](/ebooks/the-power-of-awareness/)*

Your physical senses show you the old assumption — the reality your past thoughts created. Don't let this old evidence shake your new assumption. Persist in the new, and the senses will eventually catch up.

## On Faith and Persistence

### 16. "Persist in the assumption that your desire is already fulfilled and it will objectify."
*From [Seedtime and Harvest](/ebooks/seedtime-and-harvest/)*

Repetition is built into the very title: seedtime and harvest. You plant the seed (assumption), and you persist until harvest time. The farmer doesn't dig up the seed to check if it's growing. Neither should you.

### 17. "Prayer is the art of assuming the feeling of being and having that which you want."
*From [Prayer: The Art of Believing](/ebooks/prayer-the-art-of-believing/)*

Neville redefines prayer not as begging an external God for favors but as the psychological act of entering the state of the wish fulfilled. True prayer is not asking — it's assuming.

### 18. "Your opinion of yourself is your most important viewpoint."
*From his lectures*

Self-concept is the master assumption from which all other assumptions flow. If you assume you are worthy, capable, and deserving, your world will reflect that. If you assume the opposite, it will reflect that too.

### 19. "If you judge after appearances, you will never escape from your present conditions."
*From [Freedom for All](/ebooks/freedom-for-all/)*

Reacting to current reality keeps you locked in it. You must learn to live from the *imagined* reality while the *current* reality still contradicts it. This is the test of faith that Neville returns to again and again.

### 20. "Man is all imagination."
*From [Awakened Imagination](/ebooks/awakened-imagination/)*

This is Neville's ultimate statement about human nature. You are not a physical body that happens to have imagination. You are imagination itself, temporarily experiencing a physical body. When you truly understand this, everything changes.
