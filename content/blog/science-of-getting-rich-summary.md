*The Science of Getting Rich* by Wallace D. Wattles (1910) is one of the most influential wealth-creation books ever written. It directly inspired Rhonda Byrne to create *The Secret*, and its principles echo through nearly every modern manifestation and prosperity teaching.

Despite being over a century old, its core message remains radical: **getting rich is not a matter of environment, talent, or luck — it is a science.** There is a "Certain Way" of thinking and acting that, when followed, produces wealth as surely as following a recipe produces a cake.

## The Core Principle

Wattles builds his entire system on one foundational idea:

> "There is a thinking stuff from which all things are made, and which, in its original state, permeates, penetrates, and fills the interspaces of the universe. A thought, in this substance, produces the thing that is imaged by the thought."

In modern terms: consciousness interacts with reality at a fundamental level. What you consistently think about and feel, you attract and create.

## Key Principles

### 1. Getting Rich Is Your Right

Wattles opens with a bold statement: **wanting to be rich is perfectly natural and good.** Poverty is not virtuous. You cannot live a full, satisfying life without adequate resources. The desire for wealth is simply the desire for a fuller, richer expression of life.

This was revolutionary in 1910 and still challenges many people's unconscious beliefs about money today.

### 2. Think in the "Certain Way"

The heart of Wattles' system is what he calls thinking in "a Certain Way":

1. **Form a clear mental image** of what you want
2. **Hold that image with unwavering faith** that it is already yours
3. **Feel deep gratitude** as if you have already received it
4. **Act on every opportunity** that presents itself

This is remarkably similar to Neville Goddard's techniques, which came decades later. The clear image + faith + feeling + action formula is the backbone of virtually all manifestation teachings.

### 3. Gratitude Is Essential

> "The grateful mind is constantly fixed upon the best; therefore it tends to become the best."

Wattles emphasizes gratitude not as a moral virtue but as a **practical tool**. Gratitude keeps your mind focused on abundance rather than lack. It maintains your connection to "the thinking stuff" and keeps you in a creative rather than competitive mindset.

### 4. Create, Don't Compete

One of Wattles' most important distinctions is between the **creative mind** and the **competitive mind**:

- The **competitive mind** sees wealth as a fixed pie. For you to get more, someone else must get less. This mindset produces anxiety, fear, and unethical behavior.
- The **creative mind** knows that wealth can be *created* from the "thinking stuff." There is more than enough for everyone. Your gain doesn't require anyone else's loss.

This shift from competition to creation is perhaps the book's most valuable insight.

### 5. Act Now, Where You Are

Wattles is not just a theorist. He insists that mental work must be accompanied by **physical action**:

> "By thought, the thing you want is brought to you; by action you receive it."

You don't need to wait for perfect conditions. Start where you are, with what you have. Do each day's work in the most efficient way possible, giving more in "use value" than you receive in "cash value." This creates an imbalance that the universe must correct — by bringing you more.

### 6. The Advancing Person

Wattles introduces the concept of the "advancing person" — someone who gives the impression of increase to everyone they interact with. When people feel that interacting with you makes their life better, they naturally want to do more business with you.

This isn't manipulation. It's a natural consequence of the creative mindset: you're focused on *creating value*, not extracting it.

## How to Apply These Principles Today

1. **Write a clear vision** of the wealthy life you want — be specific
2. **Read it every morning and evening**, and visualize it with feeling
3. **Practice gratitude daily** — write down what you're grateful for before bed
4. **Shift from competition to creation** — ask "How can I create more value?" instead of "How can I beat the competition?"
5. **Take immediate action** on every opportunity, no matter how small
6. **Give more than expected** in every interaction
