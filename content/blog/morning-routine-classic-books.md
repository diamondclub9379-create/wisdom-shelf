The most successful people in history shared one thing in common: a **deliberate morning routine.** Marcus Aurelius, Benjamin Franklin, Neville Goddard — they all understood that how you start the day determines how the day unfolds.

Here's a morning routine built entirely from wisdom found in classic public domain books.

## The 6-Step Classic Morning Routine

### Step 1: Wake with Intention (Marcus Aurelius)

> "When you arise in the morning, think of what a precious privilege it is to be alive." — [Meditations](/ebooks/meditations/)

Before reaching for your phone, take **2 minutes** to set your intention:
- Take 3 deep breaths
- Feel gratitude for being alive
- Decide who you want to be today

Marcus Aurelius also practiced **morning premeditation** — mentally preparing for the day's challenges:

> "When you wake up in the morning, tell yourself: the people I deal with today will be meddling, ungrateful, arrogant, dishonest, jealous and surly."

This isn't pessimism — it's preparation. You won't be caught off guard by difficulties you've already anticipated.

### Step 2: Feel Your Wish Fulfilled (Neville Goddard)

> "Assume the feeling of the wish fulfilled." — [Feeling is the Secret](/ebooks/feeling-is-the-secret/)

While still in bed, spend **5 minutes** in the feeling of your major goal being already accomplished. Don't visualize from lack — feel as if you're remembering something wonderful that already happened.

This sets your subconscious "compass" for the day. Your reticular activating system begins filtering for opportunities aligned with your assumption.

### Step 3: Guard Your Thoughts (James Allen)

> "A man is literally what he thinks, his character being the complete sum of all his thoughts." — [As a Man Thinketh](/ebooks/as-a-man-thinketh/)

During your first **15 minutes**, consciously choose your thoughts. This is your "mental diet" — as important as your physical diet:
- No news, social media, or email yet
- Read a few pages of something inspiring (like the books on Wisdom Shelf)
- If a negative thought arises, replace it with a constructive one

### Step 4: Concentrate (Theron Q. Dumont)

> "Concentration is the ability to hold your attention on one subject to the exclusion of everything else." — [The Power of Concentration](/ebooks/the-power-of-concentration/)

Spend **10 minutes** on a concentration exercise:
- Sit quietly and focus on one thing: your breath, a candle flame, or a mental image
- When your mind wanders, gently bring it back
- This strengthens your ability to focus throughout the entire day

Think of it as a workout for your attention muscle. The benefits compound over time.

### Step 5: Review Your Virtues (Benjamin Franklin)

Franklin reviewed his 13 virtues every morning, asking himself: **"What good shall I do this day?"**

Spend **2 minutes** with a simple check:
- What is my most important task today?
- What virtue do I want to practice today? (patience, courage, honesty)
- Is there anyone I need to forgive or release resentment toward?

### Step 6: Affirm and Act (Florence Scovel Shinn)

> "The game of life is a game of boomerangs. Our thoughts, deeds and words return to us." — [The Game of Life](/ebooks/the-game-of-life/)

Before stepping into your day, speak **one affirmation** aloud:

- "I am divinely guided in all my ways"
- "Today, everything works out in my favor"
- "I give more value than I receive, and abundance flows to me"

Then take your first action of the day with confidence.

## Total Time: 30-35 Minutes

| Step | Source | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| Wake with intention | Marcus Aurelius | 2 min |
| Feel wish fulfilled | Neville Goddard | 5 min |
| Guard thoughts / read | James Allen | 15 min |
| Concentrate | Dumont | 10 min |
| Review virtues | Franklin | 2 min |
| Affirm and act | Shinn | 1 min |

## Tips for Sticking With It

1. **Start smaller** — If 35 minutes is too much, start with just steps 1 and 2 (7 minutes)
2. **No phone first** — Keep your phone in another room until after your routine
3. **Be consistent, not perfect** — Doing 80% of the routine every day beats doing 100% once a week
4. **Track it** — Like Franklin, keep a simple daily log
