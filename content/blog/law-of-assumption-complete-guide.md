The **Law of Assumption** is a concept developed by Neville Goddard that has exploded in popularity on social media in recent years. Millions of people now discuss it on TikTok, Reddit, and YouTube — but many miss the depth and nuance of Neville's original teaching.

This guide goes back to the source to explain what the Law of Assumption really is, how it works, and how to practice it effectively.

## What Is the Law of Assumption?

The Law of Assumption states:

> **Whatever you assume to be true about yourself and your world will eventually become your reality.**

This is not "positive thinking" or "wishful thinking." Neville's claim is far more radical: your assumptions don't just *influence* reality — they **create** it. The outer world is a mirror reflecting your inner assumptions back to you.

> "An assumption, though false, if persisted in, will harden into fact." — Neville Goddard, *The Power of Awareness*

The word "assumption" is key. An assumption is not a hope, a wish, or a belief you're trying to convince yourself of. It's something you **take for granted** — something so naturally "true" to you that you don't question it.

## How the Law of Assumption Works

According to Neville, reality works like this:

1. **Consciousness is the only reality** — the physical world is secondary
2. **Your "state" determines your experience** — a state is a collection of assumptions about who you are
3. **When you assume something is true, your subconscious accepts it**
4. **Your subconscious then rearranges outer reality to match the assumption**
5. **This rearrangement happens through a "bridge of incidents"** — natural-seeming events that lead to the fulfillment

You don't need to figure out *how* your assumption will become reality. You just need to **assume and persist**. The "how" takes care of itself.

## The Law of Assumption vs. The Law of Attraction

Many people confuse these two concepts. Here's the difference:

| Law of Attraction | Law of Assumption |
|---|---|
| You attract what you think about | You create what you assume to be true |
| Focus on positive thoughts | Focus on *being* in the desired state |
| Visualize what you want | Live *from* the end as if you already have it |
| Raise your "vibration" | Change your *state of consciousness* |
| External universe responds to you | Your consciousness IS the reality |

The Law of Attraction says "like attracts like." The Law of Assumption says "consciousness creates reality." Neville's version is more fundamental — it doesn't require belief in external forces or vibrations. It says YOU are the creator.

## How to Practice the Law of Assumption

### Step 1: Decide What You Want

Be specific. Not "I want more money" but "I am earning $10,000 per month." Not "I want a relationship" but "I am happily married to a loving partner."

### Step 2: Create a Scene That Implies Fulfillment

Imagine a short scene — just 5-10 seconds — that could only happen *after* your wish is fulfilled. For example:
- A friend saying "Congratulations on the new house!"
- Looking at your bank account showing your desired balance
- Wearing a wedding ring

The scene must imply the wish is *already done*, not that it's coming in the future.

### Step 3: Enter SATS (State Akin to Sleep)

Lie down, close your eyes, and relax completely. The best time is just before falling asleep at night, when your mind is naturally drowsy. In this state, your conscious mind's defenses are lowered, and new assumptions can impress upon your subconscious.

### Step 4: Loop the Scene with Feeling

Replay your scene over and over. The crucial element is **feeling** — not just seeing the scene, but feeling it as real. Feel the joy, the relief, the naturalness of having your wish fulfilled.

### Step 5: Fall Asleep in the State

Let yourself drift off to sleep while still in the feeling of your wish fulfilled. This is when the impression on your subconscious is strongest.

### Step 6: Live From the Assumption During the Day

When you wake up, carry the assumption with you. You're not *waiting* for your desire — you're living *as if* it's already true. When doubts arise (and they will), gently return to your assumption.

### Step 7: Persist Until It Hardens into Fact

> "Persistence is key. Your assumption, if sustained, will harden into fact." — Neville Goddard

Some assumptions manifest in days. Others take weeks or months. The variable is not the technique — it's your ability to **persist** in the new assumption without reverting to the old one.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Trying too hard** — If you're straining, you're doing it wrong. The feeling should be natural and easy
2. **Checking for results** — Constantly looking for evidence means you haven't truly assumed
3. **Living in the "how"** — Don't try to figure out how it will happen. That's not your job
4. **Giving up too soon** — Most people quit just before the manifestation would have appeared
5. **Assuming from lack** — "I want..." keeps you in the state of wanting. Assume "I have..." instead
