*The Power of Awareness* (1952) is widely considered **Neville Goddard's masterpiece.** In 27 focused chapters, he presents his complete philosophy of conscious creation with unmatched clarity and depth.

If you could only read one Neville Goddard book, this should be it.

## The Central Thesis

> "Consciousness is the only reality."

Everything you experience — your job, your relationships, your health, your wealth — is a manifestation of your consciousness. Specifically, it's a manifestation of your **assumptions** — what you take for granted about yourself and your world.

Change your assumptions, and reality must change to match.

## Key Concepts

### The "I AM" as Creative Power

Neville teaches that the phrase "I AM" is the name of God — and it is your own consciousness. When you say "I am wealthy" or "I am healthy," you are not describing an external condition. You are **using creative power** to define your state of being.

Whatever follows "I am..." becomes your reality, for better or worse.

### Thinking FROM vs. Thinking OF

This distinction is crucial. Most people *think of* their desire — they look at it from the outside, longing for it. Neville instructs you to *think from* the end — place yourself mentally inside the fulfilled wish and experience reality from that viewpoint.

### The Law of Assumption

> "An assumption, though false, if persisted in, will harden into fact."

You don't need evidence. You don't need permission. You don't need favorable circumstances. You just need to **assume** that your desire is already fulfilled and **persist** in that assumption until it objectifies.

### States of Consciousness

Neville describes reality as an infinite number of "states" — like rooms in a mansion. A "state" is a collection of beliefs and assumptions. You are always in a state, and your state determines everything you experience.

To change your life, you don't need to fight your current conditions. You need to **move to a new state** — by assuming the beliefs and feelings of the person you want to be.

### The Bridge of Incidents

After you establish a new assumption, events will begin to unfold naturally that lead to its fulfillment. Neville calls this the "bridge of incidents." You don't need to plan or force the process — you just need to follow the natural chain of events with an open mind.

## How to Apply the Teaching

1. **Identify your desire** in clear, specific terms
2. **Construct a state** — ask "Who would I be if this were already true?"
3. **Assume that state** — feel it as real, especially before sleep
4. **Persist** — maintain the assumption regardless of contrary evidence
5. **Act naturally** — follow opportunities as they appear through the bridge of incidents

## Who Should Read This Book

- Anyone interested in Neville Goddard who wants his **most comprehensive work**
- Practitioners of the Law of Assumption who want to deepen their understanding
- People who have tried manifestation techniques but haven't seen results (this book explains *why*)
- Anyone curious about the relationship between consciousness and reality
