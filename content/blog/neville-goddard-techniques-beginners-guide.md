Neville Goddard (1905–1972) was one of the most influential teachers of mental creation and the power of imagination. His core message is simple: **your imagination is God, and what you assume to be true will harden into fact.**

But how do you actually *use* this in daily life? Below are the five most powerful techniques from Neville's books, explained step by step so you can start practicing tonight.

## 1. SATS — State Akin to Sleep

This is Neville's signature technique and the one he recommended most often. It comes from his book *[Feeling is the Secret](/ebooks/feeling-is-the-secret/)*.

**How to do it:**

1. Decide exactly what you want to experience
2. Create a short mental scene that *implies* your wish is already fulfilled (e.g., a friend congratulating you)
3. Lie down in bed at night, close your eyes, and relax your body completely
4. When you feel drowsy — in that state between waking and sleeping — begin to replay your scene
5. Loop the scene over and over, making it as vivid and *real* as possible
6. Feel the emotions you would feel if it were really happening
7. Fall asleep *in* the scene

> "The feeling of the wish fulfilled, if assumed and sustained, must objectify the state that would have created it." — Neville Goddard

The key is not just *seeing* the scene but **feeling it as real**. The drowsy state is crucial because your conscious mind relaxes its resistance, allowing the new assumption to impress upon your subconscious.

## 2. Revision

The revision technique, detailed in *[Seedtime and Harvest](/ebooks/seedtime-and-harvest/)*, is one of Neville's most practical methods. It allows you to mentally rewrite past events to change their effects on your future.

**How to do it:**

1. At the end of each day, lie down and review your day in reverse
2. When you encounter an event that was unpleasant or didn't go as you wished, *stop*
3. Reimagine that event exactly as you *wish* it had happened
4. See and feel the revised version as vividly as possible
5. Replace the memory with your revised version
6. Move on to the next event that needs revision

This technique is powerful because it breaks the chain of cause and effect. Instead of carrying negative momentum into tomorrow, you plant new seeds. Many people who practice daily revision report that similar negative events simply stop occurring.

## 3. Living in the End

This is Neville's most fundamental teaching, explained thoroughly in *[The Power of Awareness](/ebooks/the-power-of-awareness/)*. Rather than a one-time exercise, it's a way of being.

**How to do it:**

1. Identify what you want (a new job, a relationship, health, wealth)
2. Ask yourself: "How would I feel RIGHT NOW if I already had this?"
3. Step into that feeling — not as a future hope, but as a present reality
4. Throughout the day, whenever you notice yourself feeling lack, gently return to the feeling of having your wish fulfilled
5. Persist in this state regardless of what your physical senses tell you

> "Assume the feeling of the wish fulfilled and observe the route that your attention follows." — Neville Goddard, *The Power of Awareness*

The challenge is not in understanding this technique but in *persisting*. Your outer world may contradict your assumption for days or weeks. Neville's instruction is clear: **persist anyway.** The physical world has no choice but to conform to a sustained inner assumption.

## 4. Mental Conversations

This lesser-known technique appears throughout Neville's lectures and in *[Awakened Imagination](/ebooks/awakened-imagination/)*. It's especially effective for influencing specific situations and relationships.

**How to do it:**

1. Choose a person connected to your desire (a boss, a partner, a friend)
2. Imagine having a conversation with them where they say exactly what you want to hear
3. *Hear* their voice clearly in your imagination — their tone, their words
4. Feel the naturalness of the conversation as if it's really happening
5. Repeat this inner conversation throughout the day whenever you think of the situation

For example, if you want a promotion, imagine your boss saying: "I'm so glad we promoted you. You really deserve this." Hear the words. Feel the handshake. Let it feel completely natural.

## 5. The "Isn't It Wonderful" Technique

This is the simplest of all Neville's methods, perfect for when you're not sure exactly what to visualize. It's referenced in *[The Law and the Promise](/ebooks/the-law-and-the-promise/)*.

**How to do it:**

1. Close your eyes and relax
2. Simply repeat the feeling of "Isn't it wonderful!" over and over
3. Don't attach it to any specific scene — just generate the pure feeling of something wonderful having happened
4. Let your subconscious fill in the details
5. Fall asleep or go about your day carrying that feeling

This technique works because it bypasses the need for a specific scene and goes straight to the **feeling**, which is what truly creates. It's especially useful when you want general improvement in your life or when you're open to being surprised by how your good comes to you.

## Which Technique Should You Start With?

If you're brand new to Neville Goddard, start with **SATS** — it's the most structured and the most proven. Practice it every night for at least a week before judging results.

If you're dealing with a specific negative situation, add **Revision** to your nightly practice.

If you want to go deeper into Neville's philosophy, read *[The Power of Awareness](/ebooks/the-power-of-awareness/)* — it's his most comprehensive work and will give you the intellectual foundation for all these techniques.
